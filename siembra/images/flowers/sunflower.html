Sunflowers



Scientific Name:

Helianthus

Origins:

The Sunflower is native to North America, in the year of 1500 it was commercialize to Europe by Spanish explorers where they used as ornaments and medical uses.

In fact, American Indians used the seed of the sunflower to make flour for cakes, bread or mush mix with some beans, squash and corn.

Meaning:

Sunflowers symbolize loyalty and adoration.

Tips and Growing Process

Sunflowers are easy to grow just sunny spot because they need up to 6-8 hours of sun, the, now place the seed in a hole of 1 inches of depth in the soil, give them enough space to grow for the branches, and watered everyday but be careful to not overwater them too much. Their blooming season last from summer to autumn.

Tip: Fertelize the soil with composte like lettuce leaf or even dry leaf.
